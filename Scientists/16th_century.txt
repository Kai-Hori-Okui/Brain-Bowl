Nicolas Copernicus
- Heliocentric theory: proposed that the Earth and planets revolve around the Sun
- Published *De revoluitionibus orbium coelestium (On the Revolutions of the Celestial Spheres)* in 1543, the year of his death
- Launched the Scientific Revolution

Andreas Vesalius
- father of modern anatomy
- Published *De humani corporis fabrica (On the Fabric of the Human Body)* in 1543, which corrected many of Galen's errors through direct human dissection

Tycho Brahe
- Cataloguing of stars and planets laid groundwork for Kepler
- Had a silver/gold prosthetic nose because he lost part of his own in a duel (often described as a math duel, though it was over a mathematical point)
- His precise astronomical observations, mdae without a telescope, were the most acfurate of his era
- Proposed the geo-heliocentric model

Galileo Galilei
- Often called the "Father of Modern Science"
- Study of falling bodies: demonstrated that objects of different masses fall at the same rate (contradicting Aristotle)
- Improved the telescope and made key astronomical observations: moons of Jupiter, phases of Venus, craters on the Moon, sunspots
- Confirmed heliocentric theory, ut was forced to recant by the Inquisition and placed uner house arrest
- Pioneered the experimental method in physics
