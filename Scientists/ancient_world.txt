Aristotle
- Foundational contribution to biology, zoology, physics, and natural philosophy
- Classified animals systematically; wrote *History of animals*
- Proposed four elements (earth, water, air, fire) and four causes
- His physics (including the idea that heavier objects fall faster) dominated for nearly two millenia until Galileo disproved it
- Advocated the geocentric model of the universe
- Tutor of Alexander the Great

Archimedes
- **___ Principle**: a body immersed in fluid is buoyed up by a force equal to the weight of the fluid displaced
- Reportedly discovered his principle and shouted "Eureka!"
- Calculated approximations of pi
- Invented the **___ screw** for raising water
- Devised war machines to defend Syracuse; killed by a Roman soldier during the Siege

Eratosthenes
- Calculated the circumference of the Earth with remarkable accuracy using shadows and geometry
- **Sieve of ___**: algorithm for finding prime numbers

Claudius Ptolemy
- Geocentric model of the universe in the *Almagest*
- His model, using epicycles to explain planetary motion, dominated astronomy for over 1400 years until Copernicus

Galen
- Foudnational figure in anatomy and physiology
- His writings on the human body, though largely on animal dissection, were the authority in medicine for over 1300 years until Vesalius
