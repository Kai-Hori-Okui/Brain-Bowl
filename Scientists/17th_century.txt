Johannes Kepler
- Used Tycho Brahe's data to derive his three laws of planetary motion
- ___ First law: planets travel in elliptical orbits with the Sun at one focus
- ___ Second law: a line connecting a planet to the Sun sweeps out equal areas in equal times
- ___ Third law: the square of a plent's orbital period is proportional to the cube of its semi-major axis

Christiaan Huygens
- Invented the pendulum clock (1656), greatly improving timekeeping accuracy
- Proposed the wave theory of light
- Discovered Titan, Saturn's largest moon, and identified the rings of Saturn

Robert Boyle
- **___ Law**: at constant temperature, the pressure and volume of a gas are inversely proportional
- Published *The Sceptical Chymist* (1661), which challenged Aristotelian and alchemical ideas of elements
- Helped establish the distinction between elements and compounds

Francesco Redi
- One of the first to challenge the theory of spontaneous generation
- Famous meat-in-hars experiment (1668): showed maggots appeared only on meat accessible to flies

Robert Hooke
- **___ Law**: the force exerted by a spring is proportional to its displacement
- Published *Microrgaphia* (1665); coined the term "cell" to describe the structures he observed in cork
- Built one of the first working Gregorian reflecting telescopes
- Proposed the inverse square law of gravity (leading to a priority dispute with Newton)
- Discovered the diffraction of light

Blaise Pascal
- **___ Law**: pressure applied to a confined fluid is transmitted equally in all directions
- SI unit of pressure named after him
- Pioneered work in probability theory (with Fermat)
- Invented an early mechanical calculator
- Studied fluid dynamics and atmospheric pressure; demonstrated that air pressure decreases with altitude

Antonie van Leeuwenhoek
- Father of microbiology
- Constructed single-lense microscopes with magnification up to 270x
- Discovered protozoa, which he called "animalcules"
- First to observe bacteria, red blood cells, spermatozoa, capillaries, and muscle fibers

Issac Newton
- Discovered the law of universal gravitation
- **___ First Law**: an object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion, unless acted upon by an external force
- **___ Second Law**: force equals mass times acceleration
- **___ Third Law**: for every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction
- Invented calculus independently of Leibniz (leading to a famous priority dispute)
- Published *Principia* (1687), one of the most important scientific works ever written
- Built the first practical reflecting telescope in 1668
- Studied optics: demonstrated that white light is composed of a spectrum of colors using a prism
- SI unit of force named for him
- Buried at Westminster Abbey
